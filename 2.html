Zamia integrifolia is a cycad (an ancient group of plants) native to Florida & the Caribbean. It shares the name "coontie palm" with other cycads.

  • pollinated by several species of bees, including honey bees & carpenter bees (Aluri, 2020)
  • used by the carpenter bee Xylocopa darwini to nest (Aluri, 2020) and used by other insects to reproduce (Kohno, 2005)
  • used in traditional medicine (Abdul-Awal, 2016)

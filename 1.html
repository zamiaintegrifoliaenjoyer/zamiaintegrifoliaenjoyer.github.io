Coontie palm (Zamia integrifolia)
toxic, cultivable

Zamia integrifolia is a cycad (an ancient group of plants) native to Florida & the Caribbean. It shares the name "coontie palm" with other cycads.

  • pollinated by the beetle Pharaxonotha floridana and the weevil Rhopalotria slossoni (Griffith, et al., 2021)
  • eaten by the butterfly Eumaeus atala's larvae (Schneider, et al., 2002), the sole host of its pollinators (Griffith, et al. 2021), and a soil nutrifier (Marler and Calonje, 2020)
  • used to make starch by Native Americans and Floridian mills before newer crops & technology were introduced (Burkhardt, 1952)
  • produces toxins (Castillo-Guevara and Rico-Gray, 2003)
  • the seeds are coated in a thin red casing, as cycads do not fruit
